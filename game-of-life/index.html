Conway's Game Of Life - A classic. Had to be done!

There are so many articles on the web on this. Here goes anyway. A 2D grid with bunch of square cells. Each cell is in one of two possible states: alive or dead.

Rules governing the cell states:

  • Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours dies.
  • Any live cell with two or three live neighbours lives on to the next generation.
  • Any live cell with more than three live neighbours dies.
  • Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes a live cell.

Random starts with a 128x96 grid where cell states are set to alive or dead at random. Then we let the rules run. Usually after n generations the grid reaches a stable state.

There are some well known patterns out there.

These do not change from one generation to the next.

  • On top we have Block and Tub.
  • On middle we have Loaf.
  • Bottom we have Boat and Bee-hive.

These returns to their initial state after a finite number of generations

Smallest known 8 Generations Oscillator.

A 30 Generations Oscillator where a queen bee travels back and forth between two stabilizing ends.

These translate selves across the grid.

Each consecutive spaceships are on alternate paths for ease of recognition.

  • Path 1: Lightweight Spaceship (commonly abbreviated to LWSS).
  • Path 2: Middleweight Spaceship (commonly abbreviated to MWSS).
  • Path 3: Heavyweight Spaceship (commonly abbreviated to HWSS).
  • Path 4: Coe Ship - It works as a puffer engine because it produces a long spark behind it as it moves. It consists of a lightweight spaceship pulling a heavyweight spaceship that is modified to give the big tail spark.
  • Path 5: Schick Ship - A tagalong that is attached to the back of two LWSS.

They shoot (patterns).

Here we have 2 famous guns. They are the smallest known guns.

  • Gosper Glider Gun - It consists of two queen bee shuttles stabilized by two blocks.
  • Simkin Glider Gun - It produces infinite copies of glider in opposite directions.
