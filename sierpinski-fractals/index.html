Sierpinski Fractals are decorative patterns which can be achieved by repeating the base pattern at different size.

Sierpinski Triangle:
Most Basic example where start with a triangle. Then have 3 shrunk triangles half it's side take it's place and so on.

Sierpinski Triangle - With Squares:
Instead of triangle, we start with a Square. Then replace it with 3 smaller squares of half it's sides, two beside each other and 1 on top of them bang in the middle.

Sierpinski Carpet:
Again, we start with a Square. But instead of 3, we replace it with 8 smaller squares, one third of its length. The middle is kept hollow.

Sierpinski Gasket:
Instead of shapes, here we use curve to create the shape. It is formed by a process of repeated modification of simpler curves, analogous to the construction of the Koch snowflake.

Sierpinski N-gons and N-flakes:
Recursive regular polygons here. Every n-gons replaced here by n n-gons at it's n vertices. The n-flake is where instead of the edges, we connect the vertices with the center of the n-gon by lines.
