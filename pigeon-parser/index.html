The title talks about pigeons and parsers.

Why does the view port have a chess board on it?

In my early days of career as a developer I came across an architect who said To really level up you coding skills, go ahead and write a parser. Or something to that effect, I really don't remember. But now I agree, writing a parser does force you you think in a different light. Because we have a graphics component here, I didn't want to wirte any parser which spews out 2 when 1 + 1 is entered. Hence a PGN parser. Get it?

PGN (Portable Game Notation) is a standard plain text format for recording chess games (both the moves and related data), which can be read by humans and is also supported by most chess software. That's from wikipedia. Now do you get the joke?.

It's not that I am a Chess nerd or anything, nor that I am good at it. I am more of a casual player. I don't know any openings. I make lot of mistakes. Lastly I am not patient at all. Needless to say that I cannot or rather I am not worthy of analyzing any high level interesting match here for you.

The few games listed here are those which I actually won. These are all online games where the moves were jotted down by the site. I have put my own analysis around them, which might be of interest to novices like me.
