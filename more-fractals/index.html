A noteworthy bunch of fractals.

From Wikipedia, The Cantor set is a set of points lying on a single line segment that has a number of remarkable and deep properties.

The most common modern construction is the Cantor ternary set, built by removing the middle third of a line segment and then repeating the process with the remaining shorter segments. We have a variation with Perpendicular Lines. The idea is to attach the smaller segments to the 2 ends at right angles. Variation with crosses. Start with a big cross and have 5 smaller crosses on it at North, South, East, West and Center. Next have 5 on each of those smaller crosses and so on.

Another fractral on a line. This too has nice variations.

To draw a Koch Curve start with a single line. Divide into 3 segments. Draw an equilateral triangle with the middle segment as base. Remove the base. Now repeat the step for each of the four segments. Keep repeating. Also called closed Koch Curve. To draw a Koch Snowflake start with an equilateral triangle. Draw Koch Curve for each of the sides. Comes out of an inverted Koch Curve. Idea being instead of sawtooth outwards, here it's inwards. Another variant of inverted Koch Curve. Same idea as Anti Snowflake, but starts with 4 sides.

Another fractral on a line. Below are two variations.

Minkowski Curve aka Minkowski Sausage, has the lines broken into 7 segments. The segments are perpendicular to each other. Minkowski Snowflake aka Minkowski Island, starts with 4 connected sides. Each side then runs the Minkowski Curve.
